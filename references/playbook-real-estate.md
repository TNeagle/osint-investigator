# Real Estate & Land Intelligence Playbook

## Property Transaction Chain Analysis

| Investigation Layer | What to Analyze | Intelligence Value |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Ownership chain | Current and historical owners, transfer dates, transfer prices | Reveals who actually controls the property; rapid transfers may indicate layering |
| 2. Price anomaly detection | Compare transaction prices against market benchmarks, 實價登錄 data | Prices significantly above/below market = potential laundering, related-party transactions, or corruption |
| 3. Related party transactions | Same entities appearing on both sides of transactions, or circular transfers | Property moving between related entities at artificial prices = value manipulation |
| 4. Zoning and development intel | Urban planning changes, rezoning applications, construction permits | Who knew about zoning changes before they were public? Property purchased just before favorable rezoning = insider information |
| 5. Encumbrance analysis | Mortgages, liens, easements, restrictions | Hidden financial obligations; who lent money against the property reveals banking relationships |

## Satellite / Aerial Imagery for Real Estate

- **Construction monitoring**: Compare Google Earth historical imagery over time to track when construction started, how fast it progressed, whether it matches declared permits
- **Land use violations**: Agricultural land being used for industrial purposes; protected land being developed; illegal structures in restricted zones
- **Undeclared development**: Buildings or facilities that don't appear in official records — compare imagery with registry data

## Taiwan Real Estate Investigation

- **實價登錄 (Actual Price Registration)**: Mandatory disclosure of real transaction prices — search by address, district, or date range. Compare with listed prices for discrepancy detection.
- **地政司地籍圖資系統**: Land registry and cadastral maps
- **都市計畫查詢**: Urban planning zones — check if land use matches zoning
- **建照/使照查詢**: Construction and usage permits — verify if buildings are legally constructed and authorized
- **國有財產署**: State-owned property records — check for controversial disposals

## Red Flags

- **Rapid flipping**: Property bought and sold within months at significant markup — laundering or speculation
- **Cash purchases**: No mortgage in a high-value transaction — who has that much cash and why avoid banking records?
- **Nominal purchasers**: Property registered in the name of drivers, assistants, distant relatives — likely holding property for someone else
- **Address clustering**: Same person/entity owning many properties in same building or block — strategic accumulation, possibly for development or control
- **Pre-announcement purchases**: Property bought before a public infrastructure announcement (new MRT station, highway) that dramatically increases value — insider trading equivalent
