# Industry and Competitive Intelligence Playbook

Dig beyond surface-level market reports.

**Source priority:** The value of competitive intelligence comes from sources that analysts and journalists haven't aggregated yet. Search patent databases, procurement records, regulatory filings, and job postings BEFORE searching for industry analysis articles or news. An insight from a patent filing or a regulatory submission is worth ten insights from a news article, because it's ahead of the news cycle.

## Supply Chain and Partnership Intelligence
- Government procurement records reveal who actually wins contracts and at what price — search procurement databases for the industry keywords.
- Patent citation networks show who is building on whose technology — a company heavily citing another's patents may be a future acquisition target or competitor.
- Import/export records (where public) reveal actual supplier relationships that companies don't publicly disclose.

## Strategic Direction Signals
- **Job postings**: What a company is hiring for reveals where it's heading. Sudden hiring of AI engineers by a traditional manufacturer? They're building an AI initiative.
- **Patent filings**: Recent patent applications (not yet granted) reveal R&D direction 1-2 years before products launch. Search patent offices (USPTO, TIPO, EPO) by company name and recent date.
- **Regulatory filings**: FDA applications, environmental permits, construction permits — these reveal upcoming products, factory expansions, and market entries before public announcements.
- **Conference appearances**: What are executives and engineers presenting at industry conferences? The topics reveal current focus areas.

## Hidden Relationships and Market Dynamics
- **Board interlocks**: Map directors across companies in an industry — shared directors between competitors may indicate coordination or upcoming mergers.
- **Investor overlap**: Same VC firms or institutional investors in multiple companies in the same space — reveals who's betting on what and potential future consolidation.
- **Standards body participation**: Who sits on industry standard-setting committees? These positions shape the rules that determine market winners.
- **Lobbying records**: Who is lobbying for what regulations? Companies lobbying for stricter regulations often already comply and want to burden competitors.

## Emerging Trends and Disruption Signals
- Academic publication trends: Sudden increase in papers about a specific technology from industry-affiliated researchers signals commercialization within 2-5 years.
- Startup funding patterns: Which startups in this space just got funded? By whom? At what valuation? Crunchbase, PitchBook, or local equivalents.
- Talent flow analysis: Where are senior engineers moving from and to? Talent flowing from incumbent to startup signals disruption. Talent flowing the other way signals the incumbent is fighting back.
