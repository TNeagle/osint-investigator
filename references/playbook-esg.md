# Environmental & ESG Investigation Playbook

## Environmental Violation Investigation

| Investigation Layer | Technique | Sources |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Permit vs. reality | Compare environmental permits/licenses with actual operations | EPA/environmental agency permit databases, satellite imagery of facilities |
| 2. Emissions monitoring | Track reported emissions data, compare with satellite-detected pollution | EPA TRI (US), EEA E-PRTR (EU), 環保署 (Taiwan), Sentinel-5P satellite (NO2, SO2 detection) |
| 3. Water pollution | Map discharge points, compare with water quality monitoring data | National water quality databases, satellite imagery for turbidity/color changes |
| 4. Waste disposal tracking | Follow waste from generation to disposal, identify illegal dumping | Waste manifest systems, satellite imagery of dump sites, truck route tracking |
| 5. Deforestation and land use | Compare forest cover over time, detect illegal clearing | Global Forest Watch, Sentinel-2 time series, Hansen tree cover loss data |

## Greenwashing Detection

**Source priority:** To detect greenwashing, compare what the company CLAIMS (ESG reports, marketing) against what RECORDS show (environmental penalties, permits, satellite imagery). Search 環保署裁處紀錄 / EPA penalty databases and permit records FIRST. Then compare against the company's public ESG claims. A discrepancy between "zero emissions by 2025" and three recent regulatory violations is powerful evidence — far better than citing a news article calling the company a greenwasher.

**How companies fake environmental credentials:**
- **Carbon offset fraud**: Verify claimed offset projects actually exist via satellite imagery; check offset registry (Gold Standard, Verra) for project validation
- **Renewable energy claims**: Compare claimed renewable energy usage with actual power purchase agreements and grid connection data
- **Supply chain claims**: "100% sustainable sourcing" — trace actual suppliers via import records and compare with sustainability certifications
- **ESG report vs. regulatory filings**: Compare glossy ESG report claims with environmental violation records, fines, and court cases
- **Certification shopping**: Using obscure or self-created certifications instead of recognized standards (FSC, MSC, Fair Trade)

## Forced Labor & Human Rights Supply Chain

| Investigation Layer | Technique |
|---|---|
| 1. Supplier identification | Trace actual suppliers through customs data, factory audit reports, product markings |
| 2. Geographic risk mapping | Map supplier locations against known forced labor regions (Xinjiang cotton, cobalt mining in DRC, fishing in Southeast Asia) |
| 3. Audit report analysis | Check for factory audit reports (BSCI, SA8000), labor violation records |
| 4. Worker condition indicators | Dormitory facilities visible on satellite imagery, worker reviews on local platforms, recruitment agency analysis |
| 5. Customs enforcement | US CBP Withhold Release Orders (WROs), EU forced labor import bans |

## Taiwan Environmental Sources

- **環保署裁處紀錄**: Environmental penalty records searchable by company
- **環境影響評估**: Environmental impact assessment documents for major projects
- **空氣品質監測**: Real-time and historical air quality data by monitoring station
- **水污染源管制**: Water pollution source control records
- **廢棄物清理**: Waste disposal records and violations

## Carbon Credit & Climate Finance Investigation

- **Registry verification**: Check Verra VCS, Gold Standard, CDM registries for project existence and status
- **Satellite verification**: Does the "reforestation" project actually show tree growth on satellite imagery?
- **Double counting**: Same emissions reduction claimed by multiple entities or countries
- **Phantom credits**: Credits from projects that would have happened anyway (additionality fraud)
